List of Lawn Care Website Requirements▶

A new website is being created about lawn care. The client wants the website give users information on planting lawns, different types of grasses, lawnmowers and fertilizer.

The homepage will provide:

  • A short introduction to the website and lawn care.
  • Internal Hyperlinks to the following subpages - Planting Lawns, Grass Types, Lawnmowers and Fertilisers

The Planting Lawns page will contain:

  • Two videos - tutorials on sowing and turf laying.

The Lawnmowers page will contain:

  • Information on different types of lawnmower and how to select the right one.
  • A list of simple rules on how and when to cut lawn grass.
  • Photographs showing different types of lawnmower.

The Fertlisers page will contain:

  • Information on organic and inorganic fertilisers.
  • Links to pages that recommend organic and inorganic fertilisers.

The Grass Types page will contain:

  • Information on two different types of grass.
  • Information on how to select the right type of grass.

Wireframes

User-interface planning should be illustrated using wireframes. As we learned in N5, a wireframe is a simple sketch that shows the layout of all of the elements on a webpage.

  • A separate wireframe is needed for each webpage.
  • Each wireframe indicates the intended layout of the page and shows the:
    • Horizontal Navigation Bar.
    • Relative horizontal and vertical positioning of the media.
    • File formats of the media elements - text, images, audio clips and video clips.
    • Form inputs.
    • Elements that allow the user to interact with the page.
    • Position and type of all hyperlinks on the page.

Low Fidelity Prototypes

Low fidelity prototypes are similar to wireframes but much more detailed, though they are also usually drawn on paper. They show the layout of the page, but they also include:

  • Text content.
  • Images.
  • Video and audio placeholders.

They are used to test the layout of the page and to check that the text content fits in the space provided. Additionally, they are used in usability testing, as shown in the video below.
