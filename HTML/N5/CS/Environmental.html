Environmental Impact

The Environmental of Computer Systems

Computer systems use a lot of energy, not just in terms of the energy it costs to run them, but also in terms of their manufacture, and they generate a lot of waste, which affects the environment. Fossil fuels are burned when electricity is produced, which produces CO2 that damages the ozone layer and is a contributing factor in global warming. Additional factors to be aware of include:

Energy Consumption High electricity usage, especially in data centers, contributes to increased carbon emissions if powered by non-renewable sources.
E-Waste Generation Computer hardware rapidly becomes obsolete, as newer, better systems are produced. This leads to a large amount of discarded electronics, often containing hazardous materials.
Resource Depletion Electronic chips, batteries and circuit boards use rare earth materials in their constructions, and mining for these materials harms ecosystems and depletes finite resources.
Toxic Waste Improper disposal of components releases harmful substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium into the environment.
Water Usage Manufacturing computer components and cooling data centers require significant water consumption, straining local resources.
Air and Water Pollution Production and disposal processes release pollutants that contribute to air and water contamination.
Carbon Footprint The entire lifecycle of computer systems, from production to disposal, contributes to greenhouse gas emissions.
Electromagnetic Pollution Increased wireless communication and electronic devices contribute to potential long-term environmental concerns.

Fortunately, sustainable solutions like energy-efficient computing, recycling programs, and eco-friendly materials may help reduce the environmental impact.

Reducing the Impact of Computer Systems

The following are some ways to reduce the impact of computer systems:

Settings on monitors
  • Reduced the 'brightness' setting on your monitor causes it to use less energy.
  • Most monitors have an efficiency mode that can be used.
  • Do not use screensavers, they keep the monitor on and use energy.
Power down settings
  • Set the computer to go into 'sleep mode' after a period of inactivity.
  • Activate the option to shut the hard disk down after a period of inactivity, it will switch on again if a key is pressed.
  • It is also possible to change the minimum and maximum processor state settings, reducing the speed of the processor but saving energy.
Leaving Computers on Standby
  • If a computer is left on standby, power continues to go to the RAM, so you won't lose your work.
  • Data for software running at the time the computer goes on standby will be stored temporarily until the user resumes work.
  • Power to all of the other computer components is either significantly reduced or turned off completely.
