Website Structure

At the N5 level, you should be able to exemplify the structure of a website, which should contain a home page and a maximum of four additional pages plus any necessary links.

The design of a website should indicate the type of navigational structure that will be used - usually linear or heirarchical.

  • Linear Design - In this kind of design, each page has a link to the next page in a sequence. Users have to move through the pages in a specific order.
  • Heirarchical Design - Pages are organised in a structured layout, with users being able to access several pages from a central 'home page'. Content is organised logically into topics, with subpages that have information on subtopics within each broad topic.

The design should also show the direction of each of the links. A double arrow shows that the user can go back and forth between the two pages and a single arrow shows that the link only goes one way. Usually, single arrows are used to represent a link to a page on another website.

Example of Website Structure

A new website for Scots WaterSport is being developed. The website will consist of five web pages and each of these web pages will have a main heading centred at the top of the page. Further requirements for the web pages are as follows.

The home page will provide:

  • a short introduction to the range of water sports available in Scotland.
  • internal hyperlinks to specialist pages about four different water sports (Kayaking, Surfing, Rafting, and Sailing)
  • one external link to the water sports page of the VisitScotland website

Each of the specialist sports pages will provide:

  • a photo of the sport
  • a paragraph of information about the sport
  • a bulleted list of suggested locations and ideas to try out the sport
  • a hyperlink back to the home page

Past Paper Question - 2019 Q18(a)

The Giants basketball team has a website.

(a)

The website contains the following four pages/

  • Home page
  • Information about the club
  • Upcoming fixtures
  • How to contact the club

All the pages on the site include a link back to the home page. The page with fixture information also contains an external hyperlink to the Scottish Basketball League.

Draw the navigational structure for this website.

 4
  • 1 mark - home page
  • 1 mark - three sub-pages: information, fixtures, contacts
  • 1 mark - double arrow links to three sub-pages
  • 1 mark - single arrow link pointing to an external page from ‘Fixtures’
